Landforms

Coastal plain & Andes Mountains

The coastal plain soils tend to have sandy top soils and clayey subsoil. These soils are low in plant nutrients and acidic.gravel, sandmount, silt, clay, marl, limestone, and uncommon layers of concentrated shell material called coquina are some rocks that can be found in the coastal plain. The minerals that can be founded are: gypsum Bauxite, phosphate, bentonite, mica, titanium, zirconium, perlite, and vermiculite. Coordinates: 10 00 S, 76 00 W.
The Andes mountains consist of several extremely high peaks surrounded by even higher peaks. They are located about 8,900 kilometers from the southern tip of South America to the continent's northernmost coast on the Caribbean. The Andes mountains are one of the best places to get minerals in the world; they provide more than 45% of the world's copper production and about 30% of the silver pro- duction. They also provide: lead, zinc, and gold.

Amazonian jungle,Ausangate Peru Rainbow Mountain, and the Andean Volcanic Belt

The Amazonian jungle takes more than half of the Peruvian landscape, being home for more than 50,000 plant species, as well as 400 types of mammals and nearly 2,000 types of birds. Even though Amazonian jungle soils tend to be poor in nutrients and in some parts sandy, lots of things still grow on them. The loss of nutrients is the result of erosion for over over hundreds of millions of years.Some of the minerals that can be found in the Amazonian jungle are: copper, tin, nickel, bauxite, manganese, iron ore and gold.
Ausangate is the tallest mountain in cusco and the fifth tallest in peru. The tallest that it can get is 6,385 m.s.n.m. and it is situated 100km from cusco. Coordinates: 13°47'19″S 71°13'52″W. This mountain is very popular for tourism because of its colors. The colors of this mountain are made up of soils and minerals; the pink color is red clay made out of mud and sand, the white color is quartzose, sandstone and marls, the red color is claysones (iron), green is phyllites (ferro magnesian), the brown is fanglomerate (magnesium), and the yellow is calcareous (sulfur).
The Andean Volcanic Belt is a bunch of volcanoes located in the Andes mountains that pass through ecuador, chile, colombia, peru, bolivia, and argentina. The soil that is found in the Andean Volcanic Belt is a special soil with unique physical and chemical properties largely attributable to the formation of poorly crystalline and noncrystalline minerals, and the accumulation of organic matter. Some of the minerals that can be found in these areas are: aluminum, diamonds, gold, nickel, lead, zinc, and copper.

Bodies of water

Amazon river & Titicaca Lake

The Amazon river is the biggest river in the world. It passes through Peru, Colombia, and Brazil. It contains more sweet water than the nilo, yangtsé, and the mississippi all together. The Amazon river is about one fifth of the sweet water in the world.
The Titicaca lake is an important source of humidity and a moderating element of the climate. “Titicaca sits 3.810 m above sea level and is situated between Peru to the west and Bolivia to the east.”

Llanganuco Lakes & Lake Sandoval

The Llanganuco lakes are used mainly for tourism. The Llanganuco lakes are divided by 2 the Chinancocha and Orconcocha which are part of the Huascaran National Park situated in the cordillera blanca. They are located between a group of mountains called the Huascaran, Huandoy, Pisco, Chacraraju, Yanapaccha, and Chopicalqui mountains. Their “elevation range between 5,700 and over 6,300 meters above sea level.”
Lake Sandoval is located not so far from Puerto maldonado. It's home to a lot of animal escicies. Coordinates: 12.605548°S 69.041885°W.

Wildlife

  1. Andean cock-of-the-rock:Regarded as one of the world's best bird-watching destinations, Peru ranks second-highest in terms of unique bird species, with new species constantly discovered. The Andean cock-of-the-rock is the country's national animal and the spectacularly coloured bird is found deep within the Andean cloud forests, where it spends most of its day jumping up and down on branches, emitting guttural creaks and squeaks t.0 o attract a mate.
  2. Andean condor:The Andean Condor, known as the heaviest and one of the largest flying birds in the world, boasts a wingspan of 3m and is the aviary highlight of trips to the Colca Canyon and its neighbor, Cutahuasi, where it can be seen taking flight at first light every day. The canyons really are the most convenient place to spot the condor, although more intensive journeys into the Cordillera Blanca near Huascaran will reward you with plenty of sightings.
  3. Amazon river dolphin:Travel to the heart of the Peruvian Amazon basin from the base-town of Iquitos to see the revered 'pink dolphin', one of the wildlife highlights of the Amazon. The dolphin's translucent skin reveals capillaries that are very close to the surface, so the more excited the dolphin gets, the more he blushes!
  4. Jaguar:The jaguar is the largest cat in the Americas, reaching up to 2.70 m (9 ft). Jaguars historically ranged from the southeastern United States south to eastern Argentina. They quickly disappear from areas impacted by people and are listed as near threatened by conservation authorities. Your best chance to spot one in Peru would be deep in the jungle at a secluded hotel or resort away from any city or town.
  5. Llama:Llamas have become so synonymous with Peruvian culture that you can find some sort of llama trinket or stuffed animal anywhere in Peru. Llamas are also native to Chile, Bolivia, and Argentina, and were used both as food and as pack animals by the Incas.
  6. Alpaca:Alpacas are a domesticated species of South American camelid that look like a smaller, much cuter, llama. The animal is well known for their wool which is very warm and soft and found for sale all over Cusco.

Climate/weather

  • Coast:The Coast experiences a semi-arid, subtropical desert climate with average annual rainfall of 150 mm. Along the southern and central coast, temperatures vary from 13˚-26˚C, with colder months in May and October. The north has a more semi-tropical climate and temperatures average 24˚C.
  • Andean: In the Andean highlands, climate varies with elevation; traditionally, a rainy season occurs from September-March (although it can start as late as December) and a dry, cold season from May-August.
  • Average temperature: Average temperatures range from 11˚-18˚C, and annual rainfall from 50-1000 mm, with drier conditions along the southwest and wetter conditions along the east. The northern Andes are subject to frosts, while the southern Andes are drought-prone.
  • Rainforest:Peru is a diverse country defined by the Andean mountain range, which runs north-south, dividing the country into three broad climatic regions: the Coast, the Andean Highlands, and the Eastern lowlands and Amazon rainforest.
